What is a Student Council?

A Student Council is a delegation of students within a school that serves the interests of students at that school. The Student Council at Codam consists of six students. These students represent the entire student body in conversations with (delegates) of the Bocal. The Student Council is built to support students by speaking to the Bocal on their behalf, generate ideas to improve education and life in general at Codam and improve the connection between the students and the Bocal. The Student Council’s goal is to help Codam provide its students with the best possible education. Areas the Student Council can be focussing on are, for example, education quality (e.g. curriculum and evaluations), student support (financial and mental), facilities, and admissions (new students, piscines, selection). We strive to have a team as diverse as possible with regards to cohort, nationality, gender etc.
